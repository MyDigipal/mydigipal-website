OpenAI made GPT-5.5 Instant the default ChatGPT model on 5 May, and one number stood out: 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims than GPT-5.3 on high-stakes prompts like medicine, law and finance. For anyone using AI to draft marketing content, that is good news. It is not a reason to drop your review process.

## What the 52% actually means

A hallucination is the model stating something false with full confidence. Cutting that by half is a real jump in reliability, especially on the topics where a wrong claim does damage. GPT-5.5 Instant also gives clearer, more concise answers and uses web search better, so first drafts come back tighter and need less rewriting.

For content teams, that shifts the math. The model is now good enough that the draft is genuinely usable, not a starting point you rewrite from scratch.

## Where AI still gets you in trouble

Here is the trap. The remaining hallucinations are harder to catch, because the model is more fluent and more confident. A 52% drop means the misses are rarer, but they look exactly like the correct parts. The wrong statistic sits in a clean, persuasive paragraph.

<blockquote class="mdpl-quote">
  Fewer hallucinations does not mean fewer checks. It means the wrong answer now looks just as polished as the right one.
  <cite>MyDigipal</cite>
</blockquote>

That is the failure mode for marketing content specifically: invented stats, a misattributed quote, a feature a product does not have, a compliance claim that is not true. Any of those in a client article is your name on the line, not the model's.

## The fact-check workflow we keep

At MyDigipal, AI writes the draft and a human owns the truth. Our pass is simple and non-negotiable: every number gets a source, every claim about a product or law gets verified, every quote gets traced. We treat the draft as a strong junior writer's work, not as fact.

## What to do this week

Pin GPT-5.5 Instant for drafting, and add one rule to your content process: nothing with a number or a factual claim ships without a source noted next to it. Build a short checklist your team runs before publish. The model got better, so let it do more of the writing, and put your saved time into the checks that protect the brand.
